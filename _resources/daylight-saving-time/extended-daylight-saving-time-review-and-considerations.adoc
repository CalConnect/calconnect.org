This document provides a review of the Daylight Saving Time changes and
the potential impact of this change on computer systems and devices. It
is intended as a review and reference for users and in particular for
systems administrators who may need to make necessary updates to their
systems.

== Background

The Energy Policy Act of 2005 extended Daylight Saving Time in the
United States starting in 2007. The changes:

* DST begins: Second Sunday in March (previously first Sunday in April)
* DST ends: First Sunday in November (previously last Sunday in October)

This four-week extension had significant implications for computer
systems that rely on accurate timezone information.

== Impact on Systems

=== Operating Systems

All major operating systems required updates to handle the new DST rules:

* Windows
* macOS
* Linux distributions
* Mobile operating systems

=== Applications

Applications that handle dates and times may need updates:

* Calendar and scheduling applications
* Email clients
* Database systems
* Custom applications with date/time logic

== Best Practices

. **Apply patches promptly** - Ensure all systems have the latest timezone updates
. **Verify meeting times** - Double-check scheduled meetings around DST transitions
. **Test applications** - Verify custom applications handle the new rules correctly
. **Document changes** - Keep records of updates applied
